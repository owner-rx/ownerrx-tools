# Deliverable Specifications

Detailed specifications for each content type. Apply brand-identity rules from the parent SKILL.md to every deliverable.

## Keynote Presentations

**Purpose**: High-impact talks for conferences, stages, and large audiences.

### Structure (12–20 slides)

1. **Opening Hook Slide** — Bold statement, provocative question, or striking statistic. Gradient text (~~gradient-start → ~~accent-secondary) on ~~primary-bg background. No subtitle clutter.
2. **Problem Slide(s)** — Paint the pain point vividly. Use a single powerful image or icon with minimal text.
3. **Agitate / Stakes Slide** — What happens if the problem isn't solved? Use data, a brief story, or a striking visual.
4. **Transition / Bridge Slide** — "But what if..." or "There's a better way." Minimal text, high visual impact.
5. **Solution Reveal Slide(s)** — Introduce the core idea, method, or product. Use gradient text for the key phrase.
6. **Proof / Evidence Slides** — Case studies, testimonials, data visualizations. Use ~~accent-primary for charts and highlights.
7. **Framework / Process Slide** — Visual breakdown of steps or methodology. Use numbered icons or a horizontal flow diagram.
8. **Transformation Slide** — Before vs. After, or the vision of success.
9. **Call to Action Slide** — One clear action. ~~accent-primary CTA button style. Contact information.
10. **Closing Slide** — Brand logo, tagline, contact details. Clean and memorable.

### Keynote Design Rules

- Maximum 10 words per slide for main text
- One idea per slide — never combine concepts
- Full-bleed images with ~~primary-bg overlay when using photography
- Speaker notes should contain the full talking points (200-300 words per slide)
- Transitions: simple fades or no transitions — never flashy animations

## Workshop Presentations

**Purpose**: Interactive learning sessions with exercises and participation.

### Structure (15–30 slides)

1. **Welcome / Agenda Slide** — Workshop title, duration, what participants will learn
2. **Icebreaker / Warm-up Slide** — Interactive question or quick activity
3. **Learning Objective Slides** — 3-5 clear outcomes, numbered
4. **Content Sections** (repeat pattern):
   - Concept introduction slide (minimal text, visual)
   - Explanation slide (key points with icons)
   - Example / Case study slide
   - **Exercise slide** — clearly marked with a distinct visual treatment (~~accent-primary border or accent)
   - Debrief / Discussion slide
5. **Key Takeaways Slide** — Summary of main learnings
6. **Resources / Next Steps Slide** — Links, downloads, follow-up actions
7. **Q&A Slide** — Clean slide inviting questions
8. **Thank You / Contact Slide**

### Workshop Design Rules

- Use section divider slides with colored backgrounds to separate modules
- Exercise slides must be visually distinct (use ~~accent-primary as a border or background accent)
- Include timing estimates on agenda and exercise slides
- More text is acceptable than keynotes — participants may reference slides later
- Include "Reflection" or "Discussion" prompts on dedicated slides

## PDF Guides & Workbooks

**Purpose**: Educational resources, how-to guides, reference materials.

### Structure

1. **Cover Page** — Title, subtitle, ~~brand-name branding, ~~primary-bg background
2. **Table of Contents** — Clickable/linked sections
3. **Introduction** — Context-setting, who this is for, what they'll gain
4. **Content Sections** — Each with a clear heading, body text, callout boxes, and visuals
5. **Worksheets / Templates** (if applicable) — Fill-in sections with clear instructions
6. **Summary / Key Takeaways**
7. **About / Contact Page** — Brief bio, CTA, contact details

### PDF Design Rules

- Use A4 or Letter size
- Header/footer bars using ~~primary-bg with ~~text-primary text
- Callout boxes: ~~secondary-bg background with ~~accent-primary left border
- Pull quotes: gradient text treatment (~~gradient-start → ~~accent-secondary)
- Page numbers in footer
- Generous margins (at least 1 inch / 2.5cm)

## Pitch Decks

**Purpose**: Persuade investors, partners, or clients to take action.

### Structure (10–15 slides)

1. **Title Slide** — Company name, tagline, logo
2. **Problem Slide** — The market pain point (1-2 sentences max)
3. **Solution Slide** — What you offer and why it matters
4. **Market Opportunity Slide** — TAM/SAM/SOM or market size data
5. **Product / Service Slide** — How it works (visual walkthrough)
6. **Traction Slide** — Key metrics, milestones, growth data
7. **Business Model Slide** — How you make money
8. **Competition Slide** — Positioning matrix or comparison
9. **Team Slide** — Key people with photos and brief roles
10. **Financial Projections Slide** — Revenue, costs, timeline
11. **Ask Slide** — What you're looking for (funding, partnership, etc.)
12. **Contact / Thank You Slide**

### Pitch Deck Design Rules

- Every slide must have a clear headline that tells the story even without presenter
- Data visualizations: clean charts with ~~accent-primary / ~~accent-secondary palette
- Avoid walls of text — use icons, graphics, and whitespace
- Financials: use tables with alternating row shading (~~secondary-bg / ~~primary-bg)

## One-Pagers

**Purpose**: Quick-reference summaries, product overviews, or executive briefs.

### Structure

- **Header**: Logo, title, date
- **Hero Section**: Key headline + 1-2 sentence hook
- **3 Key Points**: With icons and brief descriptions (2-3 sentences each)
- **Supporting Data**: A small chart, metric, or testimonial
- **CTA**: Clear next step with contact information
- **Footer**: Brand details, website, social links

### One-Pager Design Rules

- Single page only (front side, or front-and-back maximum)
- Three-column or two-column layout for scanning
- Use bold headlines as signposts
- Every element must earn its space — ruthlessly edit for brevity
